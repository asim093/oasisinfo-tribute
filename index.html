Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah. Founder of Pakistan.

1876 - 1948.

Quaid-e-Azam, a title that means "Great Leader" in Urdu, is the honorific name given to Muhammad Ali Jinnah, the founding father of Pakistan. Here's a brief biography of Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah:

Early Life:

Muhammad Ali Jinnah was born on December 25, 1876, in Karachi, which was then part of British India. His father, Jinnahbhai Poonja, was a prosperous merchant

Education and Legal Career:

Jinnah studied law in London and, after completing his studies, he became a barrister in England in 1896. He returned to India and established a successful legal practice in Bombay (now Mumbai).

Political Career:

Jinnah initially entered politics as a member of the Indian National Congress, a party advocating for Indian self-rule. He later joined the Muslim League, a party that sought to protect the rights and interests of Muslims within a predominantly Hindu-majority India. Jinnah's leadership in the Muslim League, especially his role in the 1940 Lahore Resolution, played a pivotal role in the demand for a separate Muslim state.

Founding of Pakistan:

Jinnah's efforts and leadership culminated in the creation of Pakistan on August 14, 1947, as a separate nation for Muslims. He became Pakistan's first Governor-General and later its first President.

Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah's health deteriorated due to his lifelong struggle for Pakistan's independence and his work as its leader. He passed away on September 11, 1948, in Karachi, just one year after Pakistan's creation.

Quaid-e-Azam is widely revered in Pakistan as the Father of the Nation. His vision and leadership played a crucial role in the establishment of Pakistan as an independent nation for Muslims. Jinnah's principles of unity, faith, and discipline remain fundamental to the Pakistani national ethos. Muhammad Ali Jinnah's life and work are celebrated annually on December 25th, his birthday, in Pakistan. His legacy continues to shape the nation's history and identity.
